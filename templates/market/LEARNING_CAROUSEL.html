Vel' d'Hiv' Roundup

The Vel' d'Hiv' Roundup ( [vel ˈdiv] vell-DEEV; from French: Rafle du Vel' d'Hiv', an abbreviation of Rafle du Vélodrome d'Hiver) was a mass arrest of foreign Jewish families by French police and gendarmes at the behest of the German authorities, that took place in Paris on 16 and 17 July 1942. According to records of the Préfecture de Police, 13,152 Jews were arrested,[1] including more than 4,000 children.[2]

They were held at the Vélodrome d'Hiver (lit. 'Winter Stadium'; known as "Vel’ d’Hiv") in extremely crowded conditions, almost without food and water and with no sanitary facilities. In the week following the arrests, the Jews were taken to the Drancy, Pithiviers, and Beaune-la-Rolande internment camps,[2] before being shipped in rail cattle cars to Auschwitz for their mass murder.
